# Immunology & Pathology — High-Yield Notes

## Essential CD Markers (Immuno-Phenotyping)
* **HSC (Hematopoietic Stem Cells):** CD34+.
* **All T-cells:** CD3+.
* **Helper T-cells:** CD4+; **Cytotoxic T-cells:** CD8+.
* **B-cells:** CD19+, CD20+, CD21+ (Receptor for EBV).
* **NK Cells:** CD16+ (FcγRIII), CD56+.
* **Monocytes/Macrophages:** CD14+.
* **Reed-Sternberg Cells (HL):** CD15+, CD30+ (Except for Nodular Lymphocyte Predominant type, which is CD20+).
* **Hairy Cell Leukemia:** CD11c, CD25, CD103+.
* **Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria (PNH):** Deficiency of CD55 (DAF) and CD59 (MIRL).

## The "Interleukin" Quick-Sheet
* **IL-1:** Endogenous pyrogen (Fever); activates endothelium to express adhesion molecules.
* **IL-2:** T-cell growth factor (Stimulates T, B, and NK cells).
* **IL-3:** Supports growth and differentiation of bone marrow stem cells (Functions like GM-CSF).
* **IL-4:** Induces **IgE** class switching; promotes Th2 differentiation.
* **IL-5:** Promotes **IgA** class switching; activates **Eosinophils**.
* **IL-6:** Stimulates **Acute Phase Reactants** (CRP); induces Fever.
* **IL-8:** Major **Chemotactic factor** for Neutrophils ("Clean up on aisle 8").
* **IL-10 & TGF-β:** Anti-inflammatory cytokines; inhibit Th1 response.
* **IL-12:** Activates NK cells; induces Th1 differentiation.

## Molecular Biology High-Yields

### Blotting Techniques
| Technique | Detects |
|---|---|
| **S**outhern | **D**NA |
| **N**orthern | **R**NA |
| **W**estern | **P**rotein |
| **South-Western** | DNA-Binding Proteins (e.g., Transcription factors) |

*Mnemonic: **SNOW DROP***

### PCR & Sequencing
* **RT-PCR:** Detects RNA (converts to cDNA first). Used for COVID-19, HIV viral load.
* **Real-Time PCR (qPCR):** Quantitative; measures the amount of DNA in real-time.
* **Sanger Sequencing:** Gold standard for single-gene sequencing (uses dideoxynucleotides/ddNTPs).
* **FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization):** Best for detecting specific chromosomal translocations (e.g., t(9;22) in CML).

### Important Gene Mutations
* **p53:** Most common mutation in human cancers (Li-Fraumeni Syndrome).
* **RB Gene:** Chromosome 13q; Knudson's two-hit hypothesis.
* **BRCA 1/2:** DNA repair (Homologous recombination).
* **APC Gene:** Colorectal cancer (Wnt signaling pathway).

## Hypersensitivity Reactions (Gell & Coombs)
* **Type I:** Immediate (IgE mediated); e.g., Anaphylaxis, Asthma.
* **Type II:** Cytotoxic (IgG/IgM against fixed antigens); e.g., Goodpasture, Rheumatic Fever.
* **Type III:** Immune Complex (Ag-Ab complexes); e.g., SLE, Serum Sickness, Arthus Reaction.
* **Type IV:** Delayed-type (T-cell mediated); e.g., Mantoux test, Contact dermatitis.
